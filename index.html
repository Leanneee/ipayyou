2-Factor Authentication
iPayYou requires each transaction to be authenticated twice:
  • With your iPayYou password. We require this when you log into your account.
  • With your mobile device.
For security, you must have your mobile device available in order to submit transactions. There are two options to authenticate your transactions with a mobile device:
Using Authy - recommended Disabled Enabled Enable View Authy Paring QR Code Disable Authy Cancel
Authy is an app that is available for mobile devices running iOS or Android (other authenticator apps work as well). Authy generates a unique 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds. This code provides additional security to your account in the event your password is discovered.

We recommend this authentication method because your app will generate the required code even if your phone does not have wi-fi or cellular connection.
Using SMS Messaging
This is the default authentication mechanism for mobile devices in the event you have not enabled Authy.

Each time you request to submit a transaction, you will need to request an authentication code by SMS message. Standard messaging fees imposed by your carrier will apply.

What is Bitcoin?
Bitcoin is Real and is Valuable!
Think of bitcoin like a digital asset with a financial value.

You may hold $10 worth of this asset, $100 worth or $100,000 worth. Bitcoin is also very divisible -- in other words you may also be able to hold $0.0001 worth of bitcoin.
Works worldwide
Bitcoin is used worldwide. 1 bitcoin in the United States is valued the same as 1 bitcoin in Italy, 1 bitcoin in China, or 1 bitcoin in Kenya. Thus, you can make payments with bitcoin to anyone in the world - instantly. Because bitcoin is a world-wide standard, you may make payments to anyone with a bitcoin address.
May be bought and sold

Value fluctuates
Bitcoin is traded on bitcoin exchanges in similar ways to the way stocks are traded on stock exchanges. As a result, the value of bitcoin fluctuates -- it may go up or down in part based on how many people find bitcoin valuable. Because you are holding bitcoins in your iPayYou account, the USD value of your account may also change up or down.

Paying With Bitcoin
Like cash but over the internet
Bitcoin is in many ways similar to cash. Your payee receives it almost instantly and is able to spend it right away. Unlike credit cards, your payee cannot accidentally take more than you send to them and they cannot use your account number to make additional unauthorized purchases.
Instant confirmations
One of the most confusing and frustrating questions anyone has when sending bitcoin is how much fee to add to the transaction so that the transaction does not get indefinitely "stuck" waiting for a confirmation.

Instead of making you guess, iPayYou continuously monitors the blockchain to see how much fee others are adding in order to get a fast confirmation (within a couple of blocks). It may be higher at certain times and lower at others. We will add just the right amount based on your size of transaction to ensure an optimal confirmation. No more guesswork!

iPayYou Announcement for the Blockchain Upgrade
Summary
Our first priority is to ensure that any Bitcoin funds you hold in your iPayYou account are safe.

Because of the coming Blockchain upgrade on August 1, 2017, we will temporarily suspend our services until this upgrade is complete. Our schedule of service suspension is provided below.

Any suspension of services by iPayYou is temporary for the duration of the Blockchain upgrade. We will restore all services once this upgrade is completed.
More Detail
On August 1, 2017, the Blockchain is going to undergo an upgrade to its core protocol. This upgrade is designed to result in a more scalable infrastructure, with faster transaction confirmations and lower fees.

Because the Blockchain is not controlled by a single party, there are multiple proposals being discussed for this upgrade. Some of these proposals may result in what is called a fork in the Blockchain with two digital currencies created: Bitcoin (BTC) and Bitcoin Cash (BCC).

We will provide you with access to your Bitcoin (BTC) funds after the upgrade.

We do not currently plan on supporting Bitcoin Cash (BCH) because of the limited support for this new digital currency by the mining community. We will review demand for Bitcoin Cash (BCC) and will enable withdrawal of BCC provided sufficient demand. We cannot estimate when or if withdrawal of BCC will be available.

Customers who wish to transact in Bitcoin Cash (BCH) will need to withdraw all their Bitcoin funds from your iPayYou account and have these transactions confirmed on the Blockchain before the start of the fork.
